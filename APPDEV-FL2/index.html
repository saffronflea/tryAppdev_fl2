TYPES OF RICE

Rice has a lot of different varieties around the world and had their own distinct yet different flavors, textures, and the cooking methods. White rice is light and so easy to cook, while brown rice is much chewier and more healthier. while Jasmine rice is fragrant and slightly sticky, making it perfect for Asian dishes, Basmati rice has long, fragrant grains and is ideal for pilaf. Sticky rice is very glutinous and could be used to make sweet desserts. While Italian rice has its foreign soft texture. Wild rice is very nutty and chewy and it is often used and mixed with other types of rice as an added flavor. Each of these types of rice truly brings its own unique flavors to different types of dishes.

CULTIVATION

Rice cultivation is influenced by weather, soil quality, water, pests, and how the farmer handles the crop. Other factors also affect rice production, such as climate change, government regulations, and farmers' finances. Growing rice depends on many factors, including weather, soil type, and the amount of water available. Rice grows best in warm temperatures with adequate rainfall, but too much rain or extreme weather can damage the crop.
